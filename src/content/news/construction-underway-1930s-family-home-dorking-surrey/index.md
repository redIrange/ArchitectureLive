The construction of our 1930s vernacular family home project in Dorking, Surrey is progressing well since commencement in February 2021. Our new layout forms an open-plan kitchen/dining and family room across the entire building, partially located in a new single storey extension. The new layout is a north-south orientated addressing the new garden layout and takes in the stunning Surrey Hill views.

On the first floor, a new master bedroom gable extension cantilevers out over the new family space and forms a contemporary, covered outdoor area to sit under and enjoy the views. Likewise, the bedroom suite layout has been designed to take in the views with a large feature window and window seat.

The existing porch, hallway and landing have been redesigned as one open-plan space with a new gallery layout, all benefitting from a new structurally glazed roof-light and window to provide ample daylight and, once again, views out and emphasis the new double-height space.

The shell of the extensions is nearing completion, with the ground floor masonry walls and steel frame now cantilevering the first floor timber-framed gable. Internally, a new family bathroom layout has been formed and the new master bedroom suite is taking shape.
