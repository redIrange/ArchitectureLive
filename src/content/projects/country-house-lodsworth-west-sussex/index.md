## Country House

## Remodelling & Extension, Lodsworth, West Sussex

The brief, to update an 18C cottage and its numerous extensions to deliver a contemporary, sustainable family home.

This historic country home is located in the SDNP and has been subject to numerous extensions in the past. As a consequence, the original stone cottage only remains legible along the front facade with extensions from the 1960s and 2000s forming the remainder of the exterior fabric. Internally, the cottage lacked a clear circulation route with limited garden access and subservient functions blocking access all together.

Our design remodelled the entire interior and added extensions, in order to form a new open plan kitchen, dining & family room to the south east of the cottage, in close proximity to a new outdoor pool. The historic cottage entrance now leads into a double-height hallway that connects all living spaces on the ground floor with the southerly garden. On the first floor, a new gallery delivers natural light to a previously narrow and restricted attic circulation.

All existing bedrooms have been reconfigured to feature vaulted ceilings and an improved layout that benefits from en-suite facilities and a new guest suite. New attic storage further maximises the facilities. Throughout the property, a holistic, bespoke interior design delivered the entire built-in furniture and fully integrated lighting scheme including the kitchen & pantry, utility and bathrooms.

During the construction, the design team uncovered complex maintenance issues stemming from a decade of misguided attention and poor workmanship, which had resulted in the decay of the historic superstructure. The project brief therefore expanded to cover the complete restoration of the historic structure and replacement of later additions to safeguard the future of the cottage. This in turn enabled the delivery of a high-performance, breathable, building envelope, in conjunction with new underfloor heating throughout, powered by a ground source heat pump, GSHP. The creation of an attenuation pond for increased water retention on site also formed a new wildlife habitat.
